Section 232 Metal Content Calculus

Metal Content Valuation & Rate Determination for Derivative Products
PART 1: DETERMINE METAL CONTENT VALUE
Starting Point: Identify metal content in derivative product
Required: Quantity (kg) of steel/aluminum in the good
↓
Apply 19 U.S.C. §1401a Valuation Hierarchy CBP FAQ
Execute in strict sequential order
1Invoice Method §1401a
Metal supplier's invoice: "price actually paid or payable"
• Total payment by buyer to seller for metal input
• Exclude international freight/insurance
↓ If unavailable
2Identical/Similar Transaction Value 19 CFR 152.104
Identical/similar metal transaction at same commercial level & quantity
• Adjust for quantity/level differences with sufficient information
↓ If unavailable
3Deductive Value 19 CFR 152.105
Net back from unit price in the greatest aggregate quantity in U.S.
• Less: profit & G&A, transport, duties/taxes
↓ If unavailable
4Computed Value 19 CFR 152.106
Cost of materials + fabrication + profit & G&A (usual for class/kind)
• Based on producer's books
↓ If unavailable
5Fallback Method 19 CFR 152.107 152.108
Derive from above methods with reasonable adjustments; use U.S.-available info; avoid prohibited bases
⚠️ CRITICAL: If the metal-content value = entered value or is unknown → CBP requires 232 on the FULL PRODUCT VALUE (one line) CSMS (Aluminum) CSMS (Steel)
PART 2: DETERMINE APPLICABLE RATES
Two-Line Filing Required (when metal-content < entered value) CSMS (Aluminum) CSMS (Steel)
Line 1: Non-metal value | Line 2: Metal value (report kg on 9903 line)

Line 1: Non-Metal Value

Base HTS (Column 1 General) duty applies
+ IEEPA reciprocal tariff (if applicable) — non-metal line CSMS
+ AD/CVD (if applicable)
Report: Ch.1–97 HTS, value = (total − metal)

Line 2: Metal Value

STEEL (derivatives):
• U.S. melt & pour (derivatives only): 0% (9903.81.92)
• United Kingdom: 25% Proclamation
• Others: 50% Proclamation
ALUMINUM (derivatives):
• U.S. smelt & cast (derivatives only): 0% (9903.85.09)
• United Kingdom: 25% Proclamation
• Russia / "Unknown" (derivatives): 200% (9903.85.67/68; CSMS 6/13/25)
• Others: 50% Proclamation
⚠️ Aluminum "Unknown" Trap CSMS 6/13/25
• Smelt/cast unknown on derivatives → must report "UN/unknown"
• Triggers automatic 200% Russia rate assessment (9903.85.67/68)
• Use HTS 9903.85.68 for aluminum derivatives
Steel "unknown" note. CBP FAQ
For steel derivatives, if melt & pour is unknown, report "OTH". (No automatic 200%.)
U.S. Metal Exemption (Derivatives Only) CBP FAQ
Exclusively U.S. melted/poured steel or smelted/cast aluminum → 0% Section 232 duty (document thoroughly)
Required Documentation 19 CFR 152.103–108
• Metal supplier invoice(s) with price/quantity
• Mill certificates (melt/pour or smelt/cast) with lot/heat references
• Valuation memo showing §1401a method (or LME×kg fallback under 152.107)
• Entry docs per CBP CSMS two-line format; report kg on 9903 line
